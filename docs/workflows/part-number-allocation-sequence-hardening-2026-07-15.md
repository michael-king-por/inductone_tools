# Part Number Allocation Sequence Hardening — 2026-07-15

## Problem

The part-number allocator originally treated every numeric `Part Number Assignment.part_number`
matching `^[1-4][0-9]+$` as sequence-authoritative.

That was too broad. Vendor/manufacturer cable numbers such as `1407362`, `1417892`,
and `1417903` were represented as live Part Number Assignment records during the
InductOne REV E electrical-option work. They are valid engineering records, but they
are not POR allocator sequence records.

Because the allocator stripped the first digit and used the largest suffix, `1417903`
was interpreted as:

- family prefix: `1` = Part;
- sequence: `417903`.

The next allocation request then generated:

- `1417904` through `1417913`;
- `2417914` through `2417916`.

Those numbers are live and remain valid. They are documented as a live exception batch,
not deleted or rewritten.

## Confirmed live exception batch

Candidate audit confirmed:

| Batch | Status |
|---|---|
| `PN-ALLOC-2026-00231` | Live exception batch; do not delete; do not use as future sequence baseline |

The batch was requested for Chandler Steele with reason:

> 10 part numbers and 3 assembly numbers for the Gripper update

It produced:

- Parts: `1417904`–`1417913`;
- Assemblies: `2417914`–`2417916`.

## Implemented rule

The allocator now advances from sequence-authoritative assignments only.

A Part Number Assignment is sequence-authoritative when:

1. it was generated by a Part Number Allocation Request (`allocation_batch_id` is set);
2. it is not a custom/vendor/manufacturer number (`is_custom_number` is false);
3. its batch is not one of the documented live exception batches.

Current documented exception:

- `PN-ALLOC-2026-00231`

This preserves the live high-number assignments while resuming the go-forward allocator at
the last intended low sequence.

## Operating policy

- POR-controlled numbers must be generated by Part Number Allocation Request.
- Vendor/manufacturer/native numeric part numbers may remain live records, but they must
  not advance the POR allocator sequence.
- Live exception batch `PN-ALLOC-2026-00231` remains valid and should not be reused as a
  future allocator baseline.
- Run `scripts/audit_part_number_sequence_integrity.py` before and after any future
  allocator-policy change.
